## What are the Stages of Scale Development with SPSS?

One of the most important factors determining the scientific value of a research is that the **measurement tool is valid and reliable**. In this context, an original **scale development study** not only achieves academic success, but also opens the door to producing a permanent work that will contribute to the field literature.

![Scale development stages](./featured-image.webp)

However, this process is not just about writing a few questions. On the contrary; it requires careful planning, theoretical knowledge, statistical analysis skills and adherence to ethical principles. Here are the **scale development stages** carried out within the scope of professional service:

### 1. Creating the Conceptual Framework

Everything starts with a question: _"What do I want to measure?"_

The first step is to create the theoretical framework of the structure to be measured (e.g., academic motivation, digital literacy, social anxiety). In this process, literature review is done, the dimensions of the concept are determined and which features will be measured are clarified. A strong conceptual foundation increases the scientific value of the scale.

### 2. Creating an Item Pool

Many items are produced in accordance with the conceptual framework. These items:

* Should be clear and understandable

* Should be balanced in positive and negative forms

* Should not contain biased, directive or contradictory expressions


During item writing, inspiration can be taken from items in similar studies, but plagiarism should be avoided. Linguistic sensitivity and suitability to the target audience are very important at this stage.

### 3. Ensuring Content Validity with Expert Opinions

The created item pool is evaluated by at least 3-5 field experts. Experts:

* The suitability of items to the concept to be measured

* Linguistic consistency

* Level of comprehensibility

* Suitability to dimensions


analyze and give feedback. In this way, content validity is ensured and necessary revisions are made to the items.

### 4. Pilot Application and Pre-Tests

The edited scale is tested on a small group similar to the target audience (for example, 30-50 people). The purpose is:

* To see if the items are understandable

* To determine average response times

* To identify inconsistencies or technical problems


Item revisions are made in line with the feedback obtained from the pilot study and the final version for the main application is created.

### 5. Factor Analysis with SPSS (EFA and CFA)

After the data is collected, first **Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA)** is performed to determine which factors (dimensions) the scale consists of. Then, the accuracy of this structure is tested by performing **Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA)** with a different sample.

At this stage, research data is usually analyzed with statistical software such as SPSS, AMOS or Jamovi. Obtaining high factor loadings and model fit indices (CFI, RMSEA, SRMR, etc.) shows the structural validity of the scale.

### 6. Reliability Analyses with SPSS (Cronbach Alpha, Test-Retest, etc.)

A valid scale must also be reliable. For this reason:

* **Cronbach Alpha coefficient** for internal consistency,

* **Test-Retest method** for stability over time,

* **Item-total correlations** for the contribution of each item


are evaluated. Generally, a Cronbach Alpha value of .70 and above is considered satisfactory.

### 7. Reporting Results and Writing in APA Format

After all analyses are completed, the obtained findings are reported in an academic style. In accordance with APA 7 rules:

* The scale development process is described in detail

* Analysis results are supported with tables and graphs

* Comparisons are made with the literature in the discussion section

* Limitations and recommendations are presented


This report can be used as a chapter of your thesis or sent to a peer-reviewed journal as an article.

## Scale Adaptation Stages

In order for scales used in academic research to be used by preserving their validity in different languages and cultures, the **scale adaptation** process is of great importance. If you want to adapt an existing scale in the literature to your own research, you need to carefully follow the following stages:

#### 1. Initial Translation

The original scale is translated into the target language by field experts. Technical and academic suitability is ensured by preserving meaning integrity.

#### 2. Back Translation

The translated text is translated back to the original language by a different expert to check for semantic shifts.

#### 3. Expert Opinion

Linguistic and conceptual harmony is evaluated by taking opinions from academics and language experts who are experts in the field.

#### 4. Pilot Application

The scale is applied to the target audience and tested in terms of comprehensibility, time and item consistency.

#### 5. Validity and Reliability Analyses

Statistical analyses such as structural validity with Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA), internal consistency with Cronbach Alpha are performed.
